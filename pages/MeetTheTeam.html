Welcome to WatLock

Meet The Team

WatLock is a multidisciplinary team consisting of over 50 students from faculties across the University of Waterloo. Our members are organized in three major teams: Mechanical, Electrical and Business/Communications.

 Executive Team

 Mechanical Team

The mechanical team is responsible for the design of all components and subsystems of the airlock. This includes all the essential systems within an airlock to support life on Mars such as depressurization, sealing, structural support systems as well as unique functionalities such as a modular structure allowing for easier transportation. Members on the team do a lot of drawings by hand and afterwards proceed to completing designs with simulations, modelling, and calculations using SolidWorks.

 Floor Panels Design Team

Nicole B, Adam C, Adam V, Julia T, Christopher N, Aliya S, Wamia W, Vlad N, Aidan P

The floor panels design team is responsible for the design of essential structural components of the airlock: floor panels, airlock shell, and collapsing mechanism. These systems are big contributors to the structural integrity of the airlock. The team performs a wide range of tasks which include the following: conducting finite element analysis of each material, calculating force equilibrium equations, constructing computer-aided designs for components, and optimizing previous design schematics. These prospective engineers utilize various software, such as SolidWorks, for 3D modelling, structural analysis and computations.

 Arches Design Team

Greg H, Kritik K, Taekhan C, Chuting W, Nube T, Chelsea H

The arches design team is responsible for the design of the arches and fabrics components in the airlock. This includes sourcing material suited for the Martian environment and performing simulations of the components under different pressure ranges. These prospective engineers utilize various software, such as SolidWorks, for 3D modelling and structural analysis.

 Hatches Build Team

Nicole B, Renee R, Sean E, Ellie X, Ashley F

The hatches build team is responsible for the design and physical build of the airlock’s door system. This includes computing the force reactions given the applied force from air pressure, fabricating the hatches, and assembling the system together. These prospective engineers utilize various software for 3D modelling and fabricate these models in the Engineering Student Machine Shop.

 Depressurization Build Team

Will M, Joey L, Laurent Z, Jason K, Darsh K, Daniel R, Regan M

The depressurization build team is responsible for the design and build of the depressurization system in the airlock. This system plays a key role in guiding air movement throughout the airlock. The team performs several tasks: optimizing previous design schematics using a prototype chamber, simulating the pressure flow within the pipe system and airlock, sourcing material to withstand the air pressure, calculating the depressurization time, and assembling the system. These prospective engineers utilize various software, such as SolidWorks, for 3D modelling, structural analysis and computations.

 Electrical Team

The electrical team plays a vital role in the physical design of the airlock. We focus on “mission critical” systems such as life support and pressure control. Our work focuses on the computerized system of our airlock and the autonomy of nearly all systems. Members of the team spend work mostly with microcontrollers, circuit design software and communication protocols. This work, along with that of the mechanical team allow us to build complex systems within the airlock that ensures safety of astronauts and their ability to focus on other tasks.

 Business Team

The business and communications team addresses the financial concerns of the team. Without adequate funding, building an airlock for a Mars colony would just be a dream. The team is responsible for maintaining the website, marketing the team throughout the University, and making pivotal project management decisions that to help the team as a whole.
